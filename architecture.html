Architecture

Innovative and ground breaking architecture is our passion. For more than 30 years, lovedecor has been designing residential and commercial spaces. These spaces have successfully expressed the clients’ personalities and desires— who they are, what they stand for, and what speaks to them. We start with your ideas and visions and combine them seamlessly with our artistic expertise and technicality. The result is a work of functional art, co-created with you, rather than designed for you. As we are not limited to any architectural style – your aesthetic taste, site considerations, and requirements ultimately determine the style and structure of the creation.

Modern Architecture Z-Farooqui Residence

Modern Architecture; the architectural style of Frank Lloyd Wright, Le Corbusier, Mies Van der Rohe, Alvar Aalto, Louis Kahn, Walter Gropius and Louis Sullivan, to name a few. An architecture characterized by its simplified form and absence of applied decoration; a language of clear cut forms and minimalistic expressions. The architectural style that brought about a revolution in the style of construction and design in view of the technological advancements of the future.

Classic Architecture L.Paracha Residence

A vocabulary of decorative and constructive elements; an architectural language, rich with columns, capitals, proportions, portico’s, portals, tapered roofs and archways, but most importantly spaces and buildings of grandeur.
When most people think or read of Classical Architecture, they picture stone or wood buildings, they picture vintage structures. But Classical architecture is much more complex and enriched than just that. Tremendous amount of detail is put into the structural and decorative elements of any building built in such a style even today; columns, brackets, roofs and thresholds all pertain to a vocabulary.

Villas - M.Jafferani Residence

Using modern materials and modern design ethics of straight lines, a simple and purely minimalistic design home is created. Simple gestures of playing with the pure form through techniques of push and pull, juxtaposition of materials and projecting planes, we create the play of shade and shadow as the building mass rejoices in natural light by day and artificial light by night.
